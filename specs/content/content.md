## Homepage

### 1. Hero `[LIVE]`
- **Headline:** The operating system for robotics companies.
- **Subhead:** Robotics companies run on humans, machines, and agents. Axona is the AI-native system
  that unifies all three — starting with an agentic procurement & build co-pilot that gets your line
  the parts it needs, on time, with a record of every unit you ship.
- **Primary button:** Request access

- **Micro-trust line:** Working with a first cohort of design partners in humanoid and defense robotics.

### 2. The problem `[DRAFT]`
- **Heading:** Robotics scales on hardware. Operations still run on spreadsheets.
- **Body:** Sourcing long-lead components, absorbing weekly BOM changes, tracking which parts and
  firmware went into which unit, and keeping fielded fleets up — most of it lives in spreadsheets,
  email, and ERPs built for a human-only workforce. The result: ramps slip by months and your best
  engineers spend their week chasing parts.

### 3. The product / wedge `[DRAFT]`
- **Heading:** An agentic procurement & build co-pilot. You approve; it remembers.
- **Card 1 — Source without the grind.** Axona drafts POs and RFQs, watches lead times, and flags
  supply risk before it stops the line.
- **Card 2 — Know every unit.** Per-unit build genealogy: which parts, serials, and firmware went
  into which robot — captured as you build, not reconstructed after.
- **Card 3 — It compounds.** Every approval, exception, and build teaches the system. Operational
  memory that gets sharper with each unit — the opposite of a static ERP.
- **Trust line:** The AI proposes; a human approves; every action is auditable — inputs, output,
  model, confidence, approver.

### 4. How it works (4-layer architecture) `[DRAFT]`
- **Heading:** One system, four layers.
- **Foundation** — connect your data, tools, and ontology (ERP/PLM/MES, email, chat).
- **Intelligence spine** — specialized models + memory + agents tuned on your robotics data.
- **Domain apps** — procurement first, then quality, maintenance, logistics.
- **Vertical editions** — packaged for your kind of robot.

### 5. The three pillars — Primitives × Domains × Verticals `[DRAFT]`
The core model, as framed to Julia. The four layers above are how these pillars are implemented; this
is how the product scales.
- **Heading:** Built once. Composed everywhere.
- **Framing line:** Build the primitives once → compose them into domain workflows → package per vertical.
- **Pillar 1 — Primitives (the elements):** SOPs, documents, data, agents, humans, machines (fixed +
  mobile), inventory, meetings, integrations, interfaces. Agents are self-improving, with skills,
  context, memory, and multimodal, multi-cloud reach.
- **Pillar 2 — Domains (the functions):** Procurement (the wedge), then manufacturing, quality &
  testing, logistics, field service, R&D, IT/security, sales, marketing.
- **Pillar 3 — Verticals (the markets):** Humanoids (first), defense, logistics, manufacturing,
  construction, healthcare, space, automotive.

## Accessibility page `[LIVE]`
- **Title:** Accessibility
- **Intro:** Axona is committed to making this website usable for everyone, including people who rely
  on assistive technologies. This statement describes the standard we hold the site to and how to
  reach us if something gets in your way.
- **Conformance target:** WCAG 2.2 Level AA
- **Conformance status.** We design and build this site to meet the Web Content Accessibility
  Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2 at Level AA. We treat AA as a baseline, not a ceiling. The site is pre-launch
  and under active development. New pages are reviewed against WCAG 2.2 AA before they ship, and we
  re-check existing pages as they change.
- **Measures we take.** Full keyboard operability with a visible focus indicator and a
  skip-to-content link, semantic HTML with ARIA only where it adds meaning, colour contrast that
  meets AA in both light and dark themes, and support for reduced-motion preferences. Accessibility
  is part of our definition of done: every page passes an accessibility review before it is
  considered complete.
- **Known limitations.** As a site in active development, some pages and components are still being
  built and reviewed. Where we know of a gap, we prioritise fixing it. If you find a barrier that is
  not listed here, please tell us — your report helps us fix it faster.
- **Feedback and contact.** If you encounter an accessibility barrier on this site, contact us and we
  will work to resolve it. Please include the page address and a short description of the problem.
